## Overview

A week split between external engagements (seminars, community fieldwork) and independent technical research. Broadened exposure to AI productivity tools and real-world system requirements.

## Day-by-Day

**Feb 16** — Attended the **Innovation 2026 Campus Series** seminar at UEP Library Extension. Learned about AI productivity tools — specifically **NotebookLM** for research summarization and **Gamma** for AI-assisted presentation generation.

**Feb 17** — Conducted field interviews with residents of **Mapandan, Pangasinan** as part of the community needs assessment for the PandanChain DMS project.

**Feb 18** — Transcribed all audio recordings from the community interviews into clear, structured written documentation for the team's research base.

**Feb 19** — Researched **BIR (Bureau of Internal Revenue) tax integration** for Philippine payroll systems. Studied Forms 1601-C and 2316, alphanumeric tax codes, and reporting requirements.

**Feb 20** — Continued deepening Node.js knowledge through structured tutorials and hands-on coding exercises.

## Key Learnings

- Real-world research involves going out and talking to actual stakeholders — not just reading documentation
- Philippine payroll compliance (BIR 1601-C, 2316) has specific quirks different from general tax logic
- NotebookLM is genuinely useful for organizing large bodies of research

## Reflection

The field interview experience was eye-opening. It reinforced that the systems we build as developers must actually solve problems for real people — not just pass technical requirements.
